MORE ABOUT ME

I was born in a charming family. My parents supported me and held positive opinions about my thoughts all the time. I’m really grateful that they always encouraged me to do things that I really liked, so I had a very good time during my childhood.

After stepping into the middle school, here came more pressure about study and life, since I was the kind of person who has exact demands on myself. To relax my intense mind at the spare time, I began to make little stuff by hand and then gave them to my friends. It began with simple forms such as cards or albums and then moved to leather, woods, and pop-up books. As I gave my presents to people around me, seeing their happy faces could always cheer me up. Making fun stuff by hand occupied a special place in my mind, which connected materials to my sentiment.

As I kept making stuff, in my high school years, I made some edited videos and live demos using Prezi or PowerPoint. I also designed an English learning website in my last year in high school. My mom often told me that my works really convinced her that digital media art would be a great area for me to explore. After graduation, I succeeded in getting into the School of Animation and Digital Arts at the Communication University of China.

During my undergraduate years, I acted as a leader in several projects. In teamwork, I advocated that a positive ambiance during making process would make a product carry such positivity. So I always try to create a happy atmosphere in the teamwork. Also, by communicating with my friends and team members, I realized that not everyone is in the same happy life as me. Thus, I began to value the importance of doing research to find out what users really need as well as the best way to deal with their problems. During that period, I was thinking: why or why not an artwork cannot at the same time be an object with a function in the realm of daily life? My answer is most positive that is that an artwork combining techniques can definitely improve people’s lives in the most practical ways. With code or technique based new media art, this once seemingly impossible compound becomes possible in nowadays.

During my undergraduate level, the class such as Creative Thinking of Interactive Art, HCI Technology and Applications, and User Experience Analysis are my favorite classes. From them, I have already equipped with coding language, honed the use of software, and learned quite much about interactive multimedia art. I burgeoned a number of ideas in relative forms. I wish all these dreams will finally come true.

If you want to know more about me, you could download my cv or contact me by email . Nice to have you.
